PRODUCT CONTEXT

ContextFirst Nexus is a case-preparation workspace for qualified legal and victim-support practitioners handling complex trafficking, forced-labour and forced-criminality matters.

Practitioners may receive disorganized documents such as:

- employment offers and contracts;
- recruiter communications;
- travel records;
- identity and passport records;
- payment and task logs;
- procedural notices;
- practitioner notes;
- support-provider notes;
- scanned PDFs;
- arbitrary supporting documents.

The product helps users organize and review those materials. It does not decide whether trafficking occurred and does not replace legal advice, safeguarding professionals, emergency services or practitioner judgment.

EXISTING CORE FLOW

1. Create or open a case.
2. Record the purpose, practitioner role and authorization boundary.
3. Upload one or more arbitrary documents.
4. Extract readable text or identify pages requiring OCR/manual attention.
5. Detect and mask sensitive identifiers.
6. Permit intentional, reason-based, audited reveal.
7. Generate source-linked candidate findings for human review.
8. Show evidence relationships in the Charge–Coercion Nexus.
9. Reconstruct a source-linked chronology.
10. Prevent export until consequential review and masking requirements pass.
11. Generate a purpose-bound full handoff or minimum-necessary safe share.

Add the following practical features to this flow.

FEATURE 1 — EVIDENCE GAP & ACTION PLANNER

Purpose:

Turn unresolved, conflicting, missing or weakly supported evidence into a practical investigation and case-preparation plan.

The system should create a gap only when the available evidence cannot answer a relevant factual question. A gap must not be presented as proof that an event did not occur.

Each gap should contain:

- stable gap ID;
- short question or missing-information title;
- why the information matters;
- related candidate findings;
- related Nexus nodes;
- source documents already reviewed;
- missing, conflicting or insufficient evidence status;
- consequence if unresolved;
- suggested next actions;
- responsible person;
- priority;
- due date;
- status;
- practitioner notes;
- resolution evidence;
- audit history.

Possible statuses:

- Open
- Investigating
- Waiting on external information
- Partially resolved
- Resolved
- Unable to resolve
- Outside scope

Suggested actions may include:

- request a missing page;
- obtain a clearer copy;
- verify a date;
- confirm a sender’s identity or authority;
- request payment records;
- ask a non-leading follow-up question;
- verify interpreter availability;
- compare two conflicting records;
- record why additional evidence cannot be obtained.

Required interactions:

- Open a gap from Analysis, Timeline or Nexus.
- Convert a suggested action into a case task.
- Link new documents or citations to a gap.
- Mark a gap resolved only with a reason and supporting evidence.
- Keep unresolved gaps visible in Export.
- Show which gaps block export and which are preserved only as limitations.

Do not:

- assume missing evidence means the allegation is false;
- automatically resolve gaps;
- hide unresolved gaps from exported outputs.

Purpose:

Allow the practitioner to record immediate needs discovered during intake or document review and create an accountable response plan.

This is practitioner-recorded operational information, not an AI risk score.

Need categories may include:

- mental-health support;
- interpreter or language access;
- legal representation;
- immigration support;
- safe communications;
- transportation;
- document recovery;
- child or dependent safeguarding;
- digital/account safety;
- other practitioner-defined need.

Each need should contain:

- need ID;
- category;
- practitioner-written description;
- urgency: immediate, within 24 hours, within 72 hours or ongoing;
- source: practitioner observation, person-reported, document-supported or unknown;
- related citations where applicable;
- consent or contact restrictions;
- safe-contact method;
- assigned practitioner;
- action required;
- referral, if any;
- follow-up time;
- status;
- notes;
- audit history.

Possible statuses:

- Newly recorded
- Confirming
- Action required
- Referral offered
- Referral accepted
- Referral declined
- In progress
- Completed
- Unable to complete

Required interactions:

- Add an urgent need manually from any case area.
- Optionally convert an unresolved evidence item into a need for practitioner consideration.

- Require the practitioner to confirm urgency and action.
- Clearly display emergency language telling users to contact appropriate local emergency services when immediate danger exists.
- Keep safety information separate from legal findings and the Nexus.
- Prevent sensitive safety-plan details from appearing in safe-share exports unless explicitly selected and permitted.

Do not:

- generate a probability-of-harm score;
- label a person high-risk using AI;
- claim the system contacted emergency services;

FEATURE 4 — TRAUMA-INFORMED INTERVIEW PLANNER

Purpose:

Help practitioners prepare non-leading, respectful follow-up questions based on unresolved evidence gaps.

Questions may be suggested from:

- missing dates;
- unclear sequences;
- unidentified roles;
- disputed locations;
- unclear control or dependency relationships;
- missing payment information;
- communication ambiguity;
- document-authorship uncertainty;
- immediate service needs;
- translation or accessibility requirements.

Each suggested question must show:

- question text;
- the evidence gap it addresses;
- related candidate or Nexus node;
- citations that led to the question;
- reason the question may be useful;
- sensitivity note;
- practitioner review status.

Actions:

- Keep
- Edit
- Remove
- Mark inappropriate
- Defer
- Convert response into a practitioner note

Planning controls:

- interview purpose;
- topics to avoid;
- language/interpreter requirement;
- accessibility accommodation;
- safe-contact constraint;
- intended interviewer;
- session notes;
- consent confirmed;
- pause/stop considerations.

Non-negotiable rules:

- Do not suggest accusatory or coercive questions.
- Avoid assuming allegations are true.
- Prefer open prompts such as “What happened next?” or “What do you remember about…?”
- Do not repeatedly request traumatic details without a stated case-preparation reason.
- Questions are planning aids, not mandatory scripts.
- The practitioner must approve questions before use.
- Never interpret a person’s refusal, uncertainty or inconsistent memory as proof of dishonesty.
