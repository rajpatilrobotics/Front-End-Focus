# Raw Product Brief — ContextFirst Nexus

This is intentionally **not a user flow, page map, navigation structure, or UI specification**. The capabilities below can be arranged and presented however a designer believes is clearest.

## Product idea

ContextFirst Nexus is an AI-assisted case-preparation workspace for qualified legal and support practitioners working with possible trafficking-related forced-criminality cases.

It helps practitioners understand:

- What the available records actually document.
- Whether alleged conduct may relate to recruitment, movement, control, coercion, or compelled activity.
- Which claims are supported by exact sources.
- What is reported, alleged, contradictory, missing, uncertain, or unprocessed.
- What requires human review before information can be shared.

The system organizes and suggests. **A qualified human makes every consequential decision.**

## Hackathon problem

Important context may be scattered across job offers, messages, travel records, practitioner notes, financial or task logs, legal notices, and support records.

Practitioners may struggle to reconstruct events quickly while preserving:

- Source provenance.
- Contradictions.
- Missing pages.
- Uncertainty.
- Privacy.
- Minimum-necessary disclosure.

Generic AI summarizers can invent connections, hide missing evidence, flatten contradictions, or produce confident conclusions that cannot be audited.

## Intended users

- Legal-aid practitioners.
- Defence or public-defender teams.
- Court-navigation practitioners.
- NGO legal teams.
- Qualified supervisors or pilot evaluators.

The person described in the evidence is an affected stakeholder, not the direct user.

## Document capability

The intended product can work with one or more case documents.

Possible document types include:

- Job advertisements or offers.
- Recruiter communications.
- Travel or movement records.
- Practitioner notes.
- Financial, task, penalty, or operational records.
- Legal or procedural notices.
- Support-provider records.
- Other relevant documents.

These are **examples, not mandatory upload categories or fixed document slots**. Document types should not be hard-coded as seven required files.

For the hackathon safety demonstration, all material must remain fictional or synthetic. Real client, survivor, child, or private case data is prohibited.

## Document processing

- PDF validation.
- Browser-local text extraction where possible.
- Page and document coverage calculation.
- Missing, unreadable, excluded, and failed-page detection.
- Language and translation metadata.
- Document-type identification.
- Identifier and sensitive-information detection.
- Suggested masking and redaction.
- Human approval or correction of masking.
- Redacted view by default.
- Intentional reveal of sensitive source content.
- Leak scanning before analysis or export.
- Embedded instructions treated as untrusted evidence, never executable commands.
- Honest extraction failures instead of fabricated content.

## AI analysis capability

The AI should transform processed, masked evidence into a strict structured proposal containing:

- Candidate events.
- Candidate relationships.
- Exact citations.
- Timeline information.
- Contradictions.
- Missing context.
- Evidence gaps.
- Possible control or coercion indicators.
- Possible compelled-task relationships.
- Alleged-conduct timing relationships.
- Protection or procedural urgency.
- Limitations and abstentions.

The AI must not directly create approved findings. Its outputs remain proposals until reviewed by a human.

## Source grounding

Every consequential suggestion should be traceable to:

- Document ID.
- Page or equivalent location.
- Stable source segment.
- Exact matching masked quotation.
- Evidence nature.
- Support status.
- Coverage status.

Finding text in a source proves only that the text exists there. It does not prove truth, authenticity, credibility, admissibility, completeness, or legal sufficiency.

## Charge–Coercion Nexus

The project’s distinctive analytical artifact is the **Charge–Coercion Nexus**.

It examines relationships among:

- Alleged conduct, offence, charge, or sanction.
- Recruitment.
- Movement.
- Control or coercion.
- Compelled activities.
- Timing.
- Supporting and contrary evidence.
- Missing evidence.
- Protection or procedural urgency.

It is not a trafficking determination or legal opinion.

## Evidence classification

The product must keep these dimensions separate:

### Evidence nature

- Documented in a source.
- Reported or alleged in a source.
- Reviewer-supplied context.
- Unknown.

### Origin

- Source extraction.
- AI suggestion.
- Human-created.

### Support

- Exact-source supported.
- Partially supported.
- Conflicting.
- Insufficient evidence.
- Citation unresolved.
- Not processed.

### Human review

- Pending.
- Accepted.
- Edited.
- Rejected.
- Uncertain.
- Invalidated.

Human acceptance must never erase the fact that something originated as an allegation or AI suggestion.

## Human review capability

Practitioners can:

- Inspect the exact masked source.
- Accept a proposal.
- Edit it.
- Reject it.
- Mark it uncertain.
- Preserve it as unknown.
- Defer it.
- Record context.
- Review limitations and contradictions.
- Resolve citation ambiguity.
- Withdraw previously accepted evidence.

There must be no bulk approval of consequential findings.

## Dependency intelligence

Findings are connected through an evidence-dependency graph.

If evidence is rejected, withdrawn, replaced, or invalidated:

- Dependent findings are recalculated.
- Unsupported relationships reopen for review.
- Previous positive conclusions cannot remain silently active.
- Export readiness is revoked when necessary.
- Historical decisions and dependency changes remain auditable.

This is one of the project’s strongest differentiators from a normal CRUD application.

## Context gaps and contradictions

The system should explicitly preserve questions such as:

- Is the sender’s identity or authority verified?
- Is interpreter support confirmed?
- Is an important page missing?
- Do two records disagree on a date or location?
- Is document provenance unknown?
- Is a relationship unsupported?
- Is translation status unclear?

“Unknown” and “insufficient evidence” are valid results.

## Three analytical perspectives

The product supports separate consideration of:

- Trafficking indicators requiring practitioner review.
- Possible non-punishment relevance requiring practitioner review.
- Protection, remedy, and procedural urgency.

These must never be collapsed into a single score.

## Timeline capability

- Source-linked chronological events.
- Exact or approximate dates.
- Unknown dates.
- Conflicting events.
- Documented, reported, alleged, reviewer-created, and unknown evidence.
- Direct access to supporting source locations.
- Filters without hiding important limitations.

## Export safety

A handoff cannot be created while critical conditions remain unresolved.

Possible blockers include:

- Unreviewed consequential items.
- Invalid or unresolved citations.
- Missing critical evidence.
- Coverage limitations.
- Unresolved dependencies.
- Incomplete masking.
- Privacy or leak-scan failures.
- Stale analysis.
- Missing jurisdiction or purpose information.

There is no unsafe override.

## Outputs

### Full practitioner handoff

A reviewed, redacted case-preparation record for an authorized professional purpose.

### Minimum-necessary safe share

A recipient-specific output containing only selected, eligible, reviewed information.

### Formats

- Human-readable PDF.
- Canonical structured JSON.
- Semantic on-screen preview.

All formats must come from the same immutable reviewed manifest and contain matching information.

Outputs preserve:

- Exact citations.
- Limitations.
- Gaps.
- Human decisions.
- Redaction status.
- Audit provenance.
- Analysis-run provenance.
- Purpose binding.
- Required safety labels.

## Audit and trust

- Append-only audit history.
- Safe summaries without raw sensitive content.
- Analysis provenance.
- Human decision history.
- Evidence withdrawal history.
- Dependency changes.
- Export-gate evaluations.
- Export creation records.
- Sensitive-source reveal records.
- Unsafe-output reporting.
- Reset history.
- System card.
- Provider and data-flow disclosures.
- Synthetic evaluation evidence.
- Honest reporting of what was tested and what was not tested.

## Safety boundaries

The system must never:

- Decide whether someone is a trafficking victim.
- Assess guilt or innocence.
- Score credibility.
- Determine legal eligibility.
- Recommend prosecution or sentencing.
- Predict case outcomes.
- Rank people or cases.
- Contact law enforcement automatically.
- Make referrals automatically.
- Scrape private platforms.
- Perform facial recognition or identity resolution.
- Profile people across cases.
- Present international guidance as domestic legal advice.
- Pretend failed extraction or failed AI output succeeded.

It is also not:

- A survivor chatbot.
- A hotline.
- An emergency service.
- A reporting portal.
- An automated interview tool.
- A replacement for qualified legal judgment.

## AI and model architecture

Backend adapters have been prepared for:

- OpenAI `gpt-5.6-sol`.
- Google Gemini `gemini-3.5-flash`.
- Mistral `mistral-small-2603`.

The current public deployment is replay-only: it uses a bundled deterministic analysis result with no provider transmission. Live models are not currently enabled or selectable.

The intended future system uses server-managed model routing only after each model configuration passes:

- Evaluation.
- Static admission.
- Credential approval.
- Spending approval.
- Privacy review.
- Production approval.

Provider and model selection should not be exposed as a technical control to normal practitioners.

## Strongest demonstration

A practitioner withdraws evidence supporting a key coercion relationship.

The system immediately:

- Recalculates dependent findings.
- Marks unsupported relationships unresolved.
- Preserves the withdrawal and earlier decisions.
- Blocks export.
- Requires renewed human review.
- Includes the resulting limitation in the final handoff.

## Design freedom

A designer or design-generation tool should have complete freedom to invent:

- Visual identity.
- Color system.
- Typography.
- Information architecture.
- Navigation model.
- Workspace composition.
- Density.
- Interaction patterns.
- Progressive disclosure.
- Status presentation.

It should **not copy the current application’s layout**, and this brief does not prescribe where any capability appears.
